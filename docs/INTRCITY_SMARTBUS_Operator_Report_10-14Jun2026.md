### Recommendations

1. **Jaipur → Udaipur (410 km)** — the strongest pick: **highest demand** of any unserved route (82.9% market occupancy), premium fares (₹1,419), and EVs already proven here (5.5%). A short, high-yield tourist corridor IntrCity is entirely missing.
2. **Delhi → Jaipur (281 km)** — a **high-volume** corridor (150 trips/day, 92 operators) with solid occupancy (74.4%), premium ASP (₹1,599) and the most EV adoption already (10.2%). Short range makes it ideal for electric; IntrCity's absence here is a notable gap on its home Delhi turf.
3. **Visakhapatnam → Rajahmundry (200 km)** — short, EV-validated (10.1%), and complements IntrCity's existing Andhra cluster (it already runs Hyderabad/Vijayawada/Guntur routes).
4. **Ahmedabad → Rajkot (220 km)** — an **EV-whitespace** play: high volume, no electric competition yet (0%), short range — first-mover potential, though fares are low (₹688) and occupancy moderate.

**Avoid for now:** *Surat → Ahmedabad* (severely oversupplied — 231 operators, 50% occupancy) and *Indore → Bhopal* (sub-48% occupancy) lack the demand to justify entry. *Ahmedabad → Jaipur* (660 km) exceeds the proven EV operating range.

> Net: the **Jaipur corridor (Delhi→Jaipur, Jaipur→Udaipur)** is the clearest EV growth opportunity — short distances, strong demand, premium fares, and IntrCity not yet present.
